About Lecture Series




Always a crowd favorite, the Techfest Lecture Series is one of the gems of the festival. Year after year, Techfest puts together a stellar lineup of speakers from diverse walks of life, speaking on pressing world issues: a commentary on history, as it unfolds. An annual constant since our initiation in 1998, the series has inspired millions of minds till date. Listen to and interact with eminent personalities from diverse fields, glean insights from their profound wisdom and bear witness to their sheer mental prowess right here, at Techfest, IIT Bombay. Come, join the next generation of thinkers, innovators and leaders from 3rd to 5th January at the Techfest Lecture Series.

Our Past Lecturers

Dalai Lama

Nobel Peace Prize Recipient

He is the current Dalai Lama. He has traveled the world and spread his words of wisdom about Buddhist teachings. He was named as one of the "Children of Mahatma Gandhi" and his spiritual heir to nonviolence by Times magazine.

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

Former President of India

He is known as the Missile Man of India and is the former President of India. He played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 and received Bharat Ratna Award, India's highest civilian award.

Prof. Vint Cerf

One of the Father of Internet

Recognized as one of "the fathers of the Internet", Prof. Vint Cerf is an American Internet pioneer. He has received honorary degrees and numerous awards such as the Turing Award.

Simon Taufel

Member of the ICC Elite Umpire Panel

A retired Australian cricket umpire and an inductee to the ICC Elite umpire panel; simon taufel has won the ICC Umpire of the Year awards 5 times in a row. He is also considered one of the best known umpires in the game.

Guru Gaur Gopal Das

Motivational Guru ISKCON.

An Indian lifestyle coach and a spiritual and motivational speaker, he is known for having a distinctive way of explaining spirituality towards youth through his energetic talks, logical reasoning practicality and subtle form of humour.

Bjarne Stroustrup

Inventor of C++

A Danish computer scientist, he is most notable for the creation and development of the C++ programming language. He was named winner of 2018 Computer Pioneer Award of the IEEE Computer Society.

Jay Vijayan

Former CIO of Tesla Motors

The Founder and CEO of Tekion Corporation and Former Chief Information Officer of Tesla. He is known for his leadership in building simple scalable software for businesses and also advising several technology startups around the globe.

Randy-Schekman

Nobel Prize recipient in Physiology

An American cell biologist at the University of California, he shared the 2013 Nobel Prize for Physiology or Medicine for his ground-breaking work on cell membrane vesicle trafficking.

Jocelyn Goldfein

Director of Engineering, Facebook

An American technology executive and investor, she is the former Director of Engineering at Facebook. In 2017, she was named to Business Insider’s list of "43 Most Powerful Female Engineers.”

Jaap Haartsen

Inventor of Bluetooth

A Dutch electrical engineer, researcher, inventor and entrepreneur, best known for his role in producing the specification for Bluetooth. In 2015, he was inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.

John Nash

Nobel Laureate, Economics

An American mathematician who made fundamental contributions to game theory, differential geometry, and the study of partial differential equations. He is the only person to be awarded both the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences and the Abel Prize.

Mark Papermaster

Former CTO, Apple Inc.

An American business executive, he is the former Chief Technology Officer of Apple Inc. He is currently serving as the chief technology officer (CTO) and executive vice president for Technology and Engineering at AMD.

Amartya Sen

Nobel Prize recipient in Economics

An Indian economist and philosopher, he received numerous accolades for his valuable contribution to Political Science. He is the recipient of the Nobel Memorial Prize in Economic Sciences and India's Bharat Ratna Award for his work in welfare economics.

David J. Griffiths

Physicist and Renowned Author

A U.S. based physicist and educator, he is principally known as the author of three highly regarded textbooks for undergraduate physics students. He was also the recipient of the 1997 Robert A. Millikan award for outstanding scholarly contributions to physics education.

Jimmy Wales

Founder, Wikipedia

An American Internet entrepreneur, he is known as a co-founder of Wikipedia and Wikia. On January 15, 2001, with Larry Sanger and others, he launched Wikipedia. He was named as "The 100 Most Influential People in the World" in 2006.

Ada Yonath

Nobel Prize recipient in Chemistry

In 2009, she received the Nobel Prize in Chemistry along with Venkatraman Ramakrishnan and Thomas A. Steitz for her studies on the structure and function of the ribosome, becoming the first Israeli woman to win the Nobel Prize.

Tanmay Bakshi

Youngest IBM Watson Developer

A 15-year-old Canadian, an Author, AI Expert, TED & Keynote speaker and Honorary IBM Cloud Advisor, he has addressed over 200,000 executives, developers and intellectuals worldwide at international conferences, universities & schools, financial institutions, and MNCs.

Viral Acharya

Former Deputy Governor at RBI

An Indian economist and the former Deputy Governor of the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), he served as a member of the advisory council of the RBI Academy and a member of the Academic Council of the National Institute of Securities Markets (NISM).

Manohar Parrikar

Former Chief Minister of Goa

An Indian politician and leader of the Bharatiya Janata Party, he served as Chief Minister of Goa and also served as Defence Minister of India. A former student of Metallurgical Engineering at IIT Bombay, he was the first IIT alumnus to serve as MLA of an Indian state.

Sir Harold Kroto

Nobel Prize recipient in Chemistry.

He was awarded the 1996 Nobel Prize for Chemistry along with Richard E. Smalley and Robert F. Curl, Jr. for their joint discovery of the carbon compounds called fullerenes. He used microwave spectroscopy to discover long chainlike carbon molecules in the atmospheres of stars and gas clouds.
